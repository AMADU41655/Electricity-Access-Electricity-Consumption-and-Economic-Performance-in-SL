# Electricity Access, Electricity Consumption, and Economic Performance in Sierra Leone

## Paper Title

**Electricity Access, Electricity Consumption, and Economic Performance in Sierra Leone: Evidence from an ARDL Bounds and Error-Correction Framework (2000–2024)**

## Authors

- **Amadu Jawara**
- **Monica Nyava Flee**

## Overview

This repository contains a research paper examining the relationship between electricity access, electricity consumption, and economic performance in Sierra Leone from 2000 to 2024.

The study applies an Autoregressive Distributed Lag (ARDL) bounds testing approach and an error-correction framework to assess both long-run and short-run relationships among electricity-related indicators and economic performance.

## Research Focus

The paper investigates whether electricity access and electricity consumption are associated with economic performance in Sierra Leone, while controlling for key macroeconomic factors such as investment, trade openness, and inflation.

## Main Research Question

How do electricity access and electricity consumption affect economic performance in Sierra Leone over the period 2000–2024?

## Methodology

The study uses:

- Annual time-series data from 2000 to 2024
- ARDL bounds testing approach
- Error-correction model
- Unit root tests
- Cointegration analysis
- Diagnostic and stability tests

## Key Variables

The main variables include:

- Real GDP per capita
- Electricity access
- Electricity consumption per capita
- Gross capital formation / investment
- Trade openness
- Inflation

## Key Findings

The study finds that electricity access is positively associated with long-run economic performance in Sierra Leone. However, the development gains from electrification depend not only on expanding connections, but also on the reliability, affordability, and productive use of electricity.

The paper also highlights that electricity consumption alone may not automatically translate into economic growth unless power supply supports productive sectors such as small businesses, industry, services, and household enterprise activities.

## Policy Relevance

The study contributes to Sierra Leone’s energy and development policy discussion by showing that electrification should go beyond connection targets. For electricity to support economic transformation, policy should focus on:

- Reliable electricity supply
- Affordable tariffs
- Productive electricity use
- Better service quality
- Support for small businesses and income-generating activities
- Stronger power-sector governance and investment
